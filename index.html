"Impossible is an opinion"
I received the B.S in Civil Engineering from the University of Jordan - Amman, Jordan 2009 being awarded with the First-of-Class honor. I received the KAUST Discovery scholarship in 2008, and the KAUST Graduate Fellowship in 2009 from King Abdullah University of Science and Technology, Thuwal - Saudi Arabia, where I started my graduate studies as part of the  founding class of KAUST and its community. 
I received my M.S in Materials Science and Engineering in 2011, with a thesis focusing on the structural, chemical and morphological analysis of carbon-based materials ranging from Diamond-Like carbon to Graphene. I am currently a PhD candidate at the Organic Electronic and Photovoltaics laboratory (OEPV) at KAUST. My current research is focusing on Graphene synthesis and the modification of its electronic, optical and transport properties by chemical doping towards the application as transparent conductive electrodes.
