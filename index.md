          A results-driven professional with a diverse background in materials science, engineering, and project coordination. Currently, I am the project coordinator for outreach and training, leading efforts to support the scientific community in introducing and maintaining high standards of research data management practices based on the FAIR data principles and contribute to the development of a federated and FAIR infrastructure for materials science data. With extensive research experience in materials science and engineering, my expertise lies in organic materials, molecular doping, and the characterization of electronic structure. As a certified Project Management Professional (PMP®), I am skilled in driving projects to success and fostering international collaborations. I thrive on achievements, learning opportunities, and problem-solving, leveraging my remarkable leadership practice and highly organized nature to deliver exceptional results. Known for strong oral and written communication skills, I excel in effectively conveying complex concepts to diverse audiences.

        <h2>Scientific research:</h2>
        <p>I have spent over ten years working in the field of organic and hybrid electronics, designing, synthesizing, and characterizing molecularly doped semiconductors, conducting polymers, and two-dimensional (2D) materials. Using hands-on spectroscopies (Raman and photoemission) and electrical measurements, I study charge-carrier interactions and apply those insights to p- and n-dopant development, as well as to the surface and intercalation doping of graphene and MXenes. Initially, I tuned the electronic properties of CVD graphene using bromine intercalation and molecular surface dopants to boost conductivity and control the work function. I then expanded my projects to include tuning the work function and electrical conductivity of polymers, engineering solvent-resistant multilayer polymer films, and developing solution-processed transparent electrodes and perovskite crystal passivation. I always aim to optimize interfaces for next-generation optoelectronic and energy applications.</p>
